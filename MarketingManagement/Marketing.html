Case Study 1

Market Segmentation, Targeting and Positioning Analysis

Team Members : 6
CASE: Identify a (potential) market segment targeted for a weekend part-time Masters Program at University of Waterloo.
APPROACH: Market research was carried out to identify potential segments. The different segments identified were rated against factors like 'Marketing Attractiveness' and 'Competitive Position Factors'. Then, Market Attractiveness and Competitive Matrix was developed to position the potential segments providing valid reasons of the chosen rating of each segment. After evaluating and with valid justification, positioning statement was provided.

Case Study 2

Google Glass : Application Study

Team Members : 5
CASE: Google Glass was unveiled in 2013. It was targeted towards general public audience but was discontinued in 2015 after beta versions failed to gain traction due to its high price tag of $1,500 , clunky design and concerns about privacy. Google eventually shifted its focus towards business uses of glasses. The case stud aims at determining if Google Glass Edition 2 would succeed or not. APPROACH: Analysing 5 levels of product, determining new product adoption curve and how it will be perceived by Target Market, Degree of Change Behaviour needed by customers and finally conclusion whether this product will succeed or not.
